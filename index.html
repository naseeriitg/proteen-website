1. Removing the Earthy Note

Raw millets contain bitter tannins and phytic acid. We soak them in a warm 1:3 water ratio for 8 hours with Himalayan salt, rinse 3 times, and dry before sand-puffing at 180°C. This turns grassy grain into a sweet, toasted walnut enhancer.

2. Sub-Threshold Salt (0.3g)

Adding exactly 0.3% Himalayan salt acts as a flavor multiplier. At this level, it does not taste salty; it actively suppresses the bitterness of raw walnuts and enhances jaggery's sweet perception by 15-20%.

3. Volatile Aroma Boosting

Walnut terpenes are fat-soluble. We drizzle 1.5g of cold-pressed walnut oil directly over the dry ingredients. This "carries" the fresh aroma directly to your nose upon opening the nitrogen-flushed wrapper.

Designed by Nature

Why do walnuts look like human brains?

It is a strange coincidence that a cracked walnut kernel looks exactly like the human brain. But the chemistry matches the shape: walnuts are the only nuts rich in alpha-linolenic acid (plant-based Omega-3).

Your body cannot make these essential fats—you have to eat them. We combine wild-harvested walnuts with roasted millets and ground chia to make a dense, slow-burning food that keeps your mind clear and your heart healthy.

Why Walnut

No Isolates. Just Whole Foods.

Most brands use cheap peanuts, soy flakes, or highly processed whey powder as base fillers. We use Kashmiri walnuts because they are clean and nutritionally superior. Here is why.

01 — 🧠

Rich in Omega-3 ALA

A single proTeen bar contains 4.72g of plant-based Omega-3. Most other energy bars have virtually zero, relying instead on palm oil or hydrogenated fats for texture.

02 — ❤️

Heart Health Support

The FDA recognizes that eating 1.5 ounces of walnuts per day as part of a low-saturated-fat diet may reduce the risk of heart disease. Walnuts also have more antioxidants than almonds or peanuts.

03 — 🌾

Easy Digestion

We don't use protein isolates. Isolates are processed using high heat and chemical solvents, which often cause bloating. We keep the walnuts, millets, and seeds intact so your stomach digests them naturally.

Scientific References: Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health • NIH National Library of Medicine • FDA Qualified Health Claim for Walnuts (2004)
